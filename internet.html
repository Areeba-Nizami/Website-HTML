The Evolution of the Internet and Web (1990s - 2000s)
The Rise of the Digital World

1991: The World Wide Web is launched

Tim Berners-Lee launched the World Wide Web, revolutionizing how information is shared globally. This innovation laid the foundation for the modern internet as we know it today.

1995: The release of Windows 95

Windows 95 brought the internet to the masses with built-in support for browsing and networking, making it easier for people to connect to the web and shaping the way modern operating systems function.

2004: Facebook is launched

Facebook was launched, marking the beginning of social media as a dominant force in global communication. It changed the way people interacted online, connecting billions of users worldwide.

Impact on Society

The 1990s and 2000s were transformative years for the internet and web technologies. The launch of the World Wide Web in 1991 opened up a new world of connectivity and knowledge sharing. Windows 95 made the internet accessible to everyday users, while the launch of Facebook in 2004 revolutionized how people communicate online. The internet and social media grew into essential tools for both personal and professional life, leading to the interconnected, digital world we live in today.
