The Advent of Personal Computers (1970s - 1980s)
A Glimpse into the Early Days of PCs

1977: Apple II is released

One of the first personal computers for the consumer market. It featured color graphics and an open architecture, making it widely accessible for home users and small businesses.

1981: IBM PC is introduced

The introduction of the IBM PC set the standard for personal computing, with its open architecture allowing third-party developers to create software and hardware. This marked the beginning of the personal computer revolution.

1984: Apple Macintosh is launched

The Macintosh was revolutionary with its graphical user interface (GUI), making personal computing more accessible to non-technical users. It set the stage for modern desktop computing, introducing the mouse and icons as part of the user experience.

Impact on Society

The 1970s and 1980s marked a pivotal point in the history of technology. With the release of personal computers like the Apple II and IBM PC, computing became more affordable and accessible. For the first time, individuals and small businesses could own computers. The Macintosh, with its innovative graphical interface, further democratized computing by making it intuitive and user-friendly. These milestones laid the foundation for the modern computing world that we know today, revolutionizing industries and personal lives alike.
